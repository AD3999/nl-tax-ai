Known deviations from Belastingdienst
ZVW base: Our engine calculates ZVW on taxable Box 1 income (after MKB). The official Belastingdienst ZVW bijdrage for ZZP is on winst before MKB deduction. Expect the Belastingdienst ZVW to be ~8–12% higher than our figure for mid-range ZZP incomes.
AK / AHK basis: Our engine uses taxable Box 1 income as the credit basis. Belastingdienst may use a slightly different income concept ("arbeidsinkomen"). Small differences of €0–€50 are acceptable.
Box 3: Our engine uses 100% investment return rate (6%) by default. If the user has savings, the Belastingdienst calculation will differ based on actual savings/investments split.

D. Chat / AI — Test Questions

Q1 (Dutch) — "Ik heb 980 uur gewerkt in 2026. Heb ik recht op zelfstandigenaftrek?"
Expected answer: No. The urencriterium requires 1,225 hours minimum per year. At 980 hours you do not qualify for the €1,200 zelfstandigenaftrek in 2026. You also cannot claim the startersaftrek (€2,123) because it requires the ZA threshold to be met. Source: ZA-2026-001
Q2 (English) — "I have a 1-year-old child and my partner doesn't work. Am I eligible for IACK?"
Expected answer: Yes. The IACK (inkomensafhankelijke combinatiekorting) applies when you have a child under 12 registered at your address and your work income exceeds €6,239. Since your income of €21,650 exceeds this threshold, you qualify. The maximum IACK is €3,032. Your exact credit: (€21,650 − €6,239) × 11.45% = ~€1,765. Source: IACK-2026-001
Q3 (Persian) — "آیا می‌توانم برای اجاره‌ام huurtoeslag دریافت کنم؟"
Expected answer: With €850/month rent and estimated household income below the threshold, you are likely eligible for huurtoeslag. The 2026 reform abolished the rent ceiling — any rent amount now qualifies. Your eligibility depends on the combined household income (you + partner). If combined income is below ~€46,000, you should apply at toeslagen.belastingdienst.nl. Source: HT-2026-001

D. Chat / AI — Test Questions

Q1 — "Is 2026 really the last year for startersaftrek?"
Expected answer: Yes. The startersaftrek of €2,123 is abolished from 1 January 2027. If you are in your 1st, 2nd, or 3rd year of business and meet the urencriterium (1,225 hours), 2026 is your last opportunity to claim it. This will save you approximately €798 in taxes at your income level (€2,123 × effective marginal rate ~37.6%). Source: SA-2026-001
Q2 — "What is my Wet DBA risk with 65% of income from one client?"
Expected answer: With 65% of your income from a single client, your Wet DBA risk is Medium. The Belastingdienst has been actively enforcing the Wet DBA since January 2025. At 65% you are in the warning zone (50–70%). Key risk factors: you must demonstrate independent professional work, use your own tools, and ideally serve 2+ clients. Strongly consider requesting a formal modelovereenkomst with your main client. Source: WD-2026-001
Q3 — "I didn't invest in pension this year. How much could I have saved?"
Expected answer: Based on your gross profit of ~€62,500, your jaarruimte (annual pension space) is approximately: 30% × (€62,500 − €19,172) = 30% × €43,328 = €12,998. If you had contributed this amount, you would have reduced your taxable income to ~€37,564 — saving approximately €4,887 in income tax. Consider opening a lijfrente or bankspaarrekening product. Source: LR-2026-001

Q: "What is the minimum salary I must pay myself as a DGA in 2026?"
Expected answer: The minimum gebruikelijk loon (customary salary) for a DGA in 2026 is €56,000. You must pay yourself at least the highest of: €56,000, 75% of the salary for the most comparable position in the market, or the salary of the best-paid employee in your BV. Paying below this triggers a correction and potential penalty from the Belastingdienst. Source: DGA-2026-001
