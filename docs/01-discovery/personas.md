# User Personas

> Eight personas covering all primary roles in TaxWijs.
> Updated: 2026-06-13

---

## Persona 1 — Arash: The ZZP Freelancer (IT)

**Demographics:** 34, Iranian expat, in the Netherlands 6 years, IT contractor  
**Residence:** Amsterdam  
**Language:** Persian (home), English (work), Dutch (basic)  
**Income:** €72,000/year ZZP revenue, single client (60% of revenue)

**Goals:**
- Know exactly how much tax to set aside each month
- Not get surprised by a large tax bill in spring
- Understand the ZVW contribution that his accountant friend keeps mentioning
- Know if his single-client situation creates Wet DBA risk

**Pain points:**
- All official Dutch tax information is in Dutch only
- He reads English financial content but wants tax answers in Persian for nuance
- His previous accountant gave him a €4,200 bill he didn't understand
- He doesn't know what "MKB-winstvrijstelling" means or how it helps him

**Behaviors:**
- Uses Google in Persian to search for Dutch tax questions
- Asks Dutch tax questions on Reddit/expatforum
- Trusts tools that explain *why*, not just *what*
- Keeps everything in a folder until April, then panics

**What TaxWijs gives him:** Persian-language tax guidance, ZVW explanation, Wet DBA risk score, monthly reserve calculator, deduction finder

**Residence:** Utrecht  
**Language:** Dutch, English  
**Income:** €28,000/year ZZP revenue, design work, multiple clients

**Behaviors:**

- Does her own BTW returns via Mijn Belastingdienst
- Tracks income in a spreadsheet

**What TaxWijs gives her:** Startersaftrek eligibility check, urencriterium tracker, home office deduction clarification, BTW deadline reminders, IB return guidance

## Persona 3 — Maria: The Expat Employee (30% Ruling)

**Demographics:** 31, Spanish, arrived 2 years ago, software engineer at a Dutch tech company  
**Residence:** Amsterdam  
**Language:** English, Spanish  
**Income:** €90,000 gross salary, 30% ruling applied

**Goals:**
- Understand whether her 30% ruling is being applied correctly
- Know when her 30% ruling phases down to 20% (year 4)
- File her IB return correctly as a partial non-resident
- Not leave money on the table

**Pain points:**
- She doesn't understand Dutch, so all official sources are inaccessible
- Her employer handles payroll but she doesn't know if it's right
- She doesn't know she needs to file an IB return (employer handles wage tax)
- The 30% ruling phase-down confuses her

**Behaviors:**
- Reads Expatica and InterNations forums
- Uses English-language apps for banking and investing
- Asks HR for tax questions (HR often doesn't know either)

**What TaxWijs gives her:** 30% ruling calculator, phase-down timeline, IB return guidance for expats, English-language explanations

## Persona 6 — Sara: The Accountant

**Demographics:** 38, Dutch, works at a mid-size accounting firm  
**Credentials:** AA (Accountant-Administratieconsulent)  
**Clients:** 45 clients, mix of ZZP and small businesses  
**Language:** Dutch, English

**Goals:**
- Spend less time chasing clients for documents
- Know immediately which clients are behind on their preparation
- Have AI draft the initial deduction analysis so she can focus on review
- File clean returns on time

**Pain points:**
- Clients send documents at the last minute or in the wrong format
- She spends 40% of her time on document collection and basic Q&A
- Existing tools don't integrate AI-assisted extraction
- Persian-speaking clients are hard to serve without a translator

**Behaviors:**
- Uses email and WhatsApp for client communication
- Uses basic accounting software for bookkeeping
- Manually checks each client's readiness by scanning emails

**What TaxWijs gives her:** Accountant portal with engagement workspace, readiness dashboard, AI document extraction, trilingual client portal, automated reminders

## Persona 7 — Pieter: The Firm Manager

**Demographics:** 55, Dutch, managing partner at an accounting firm with 12 accountants  
**Language:** Dutch  
**Focus:** Business development, quality control, compliance

**Goals:**
- See the status of all client engagements across all accountants
- Know which engagements are at risk (overdue, blocked, missing documents)
- Verify that new rule changes are properly reflected in client advice
- Attract new clients via the marketplace

**Pain points:**
- No single dashboard showing firm-wide engagement status
- Cannot easily reassign clients between accountants
- Rule changes require manual briefing of all accountants
- Client acquisition is entirely through word-of-mouth

**Behaviors:**
- Manages via email and weekly team meetings
- Uses basic CRM for lead tracking

**What TaxWijs gives him:** Firm-level dashboard, engagement health overview, rule change notifications, marketplace listing

## Persona 8 — Admin (TaxWijs Operations)

**Demographics:** TaxWijs internal team member  
**Role:** Platform administrator, content updater, support

**Goals:**
- Update tax rules annually without code changes
- Manage firm and user accounts
- Review verification queue for new rules
- Monitor system health

**Pain points:**
- Annual rule updates currently require code changes and a developer
- No admin UI — only Django admin (not user-friendly)
- Cannot easily see which users are blocked or need help

**Behaviors:**
- Uses Django admin daily
- Runs validation scripts after data updates

**What TaxWijs gives her:** React-based admin console, rule management UI, verification workflow, system health dashboard
