ZVW Base — Key Difference

For ZZP, Belastingdienst calculates ZVW on profit after ondernemersaftrek (ZA+SA+KIA) but BEFORE the MKB deduction and BEFORE pension deduction.

Our engine calculates ZVW on the taxable Box 1 income (AFTER MKB, BEFORE pension).

This means our ZVW base is always lower than Belastingdienst's base — partially offsetting the rate error.

The Two Root Causes — Should These Be Fixed?

Bug 1 — Bracket 2 rate: 37.07% instead of 37.56% Impact per scenario: €45–€194. Affects every user with income between €38,883–€78,426.

To fix: in engine.py line 43, change b2_rate = 0.3707 to b2_rate = 0.3756. One line.
Bug 2 — ZVW: 5.32%/€71,628 instead of 4.85%/€79,409 Impact: €40–€119 for ZZP scenarios. Phase 16 "correction" introduced the 2024 rate (5.32%) instead of the correct 2026 rate (4.85%). The original value (4.85%/€79,409) was actually correct.

To fix: in tax_rules_2026.json rule ZVW-2026-001: set "value": 4.85 and "ceiling_income": 79409. Also fix engine.py line 190 comment.
Both bugs cause us to give users a LOWER tax estimate than they will actually owe. This is the safer direction (users keep more reserve), but ideally both should be corrected before launch so users get an accurate picture.
